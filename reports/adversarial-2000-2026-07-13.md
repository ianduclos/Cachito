# Adversarial policy league — 2,000 games

Date: 2026-07-13  
Seed: `20260713`

## Run setup

- 2 players: 667 games
- 4 players: 667 games
- 6 players: 666 games
- Average game: 54.96 actions and 18.00 rounds
- Six fixed bot styles competed with rotating seats and policy combinations.
- The policies did not update their parameters during this run; this is adversarial self-play evaluation, not yet self-learning.

The performance ratio compares actual wins with the number expected if every policy were equally strong. A value above 1.00 is above fair share.

## Interpretation

Conservative and Survivalist are effectively tied at the top. Conservative has the strongest overall result, the best Dudo accuracy, and the best-calibrated bid probabilities. Survivalist wins slightly more often at four-player tables despite never using Calzo.

Aggressive bluffing is strongly punished as table size grows. Bluffer falls to a 4.5% win rate with six players. Challenger's low Dudo threshold also underperforms, suggesting the current bot should demand more evidence before challenging.

Calzo is not automatically valuable even when it is often correct. Exact seeker hit 73.4% of 207 Calzos but still finished below fair share, consistent with the two-die penalty making a merely good Calzo too expensive in the wrong game states.

## Recommended next experiment

Use Conservative and Survivalist as parents for a parameter-search league. Mutate thresholds and bluff rates in small steps, evaluate each candidate across all three table sizes and rotating seats, and retain candidates on held-out seeds. Score both overall strength and worst-table-size performance so optimization does not create a specialist that fails at another player count.
